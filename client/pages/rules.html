Basic Rules

1. Game Objective

Sink the enemy fleet before they sink yours!

2. Game Components

Each player has a 10x10 gameboard. Player will have two boards, one where they can view their fleet and another which represents the enemy's field, showing missed shots and hits to the enemy's fleet.

The fleet:

  • 1 aircraft carrier (5 squares, either horizontal or vertical)
  • 1 battleship (4 squares, either horizontal or vertical)
  • 1 cruise ship (3 squares, either horizontal or vertical)
  • 1 submarine (3 squares, either horizontal or vertical)
  • 1 destroyer (2 squares, either horizontal or vertical)
3. Preparation

Each player must place thier boats in their field, making sure the other players don't see your fleet placement.

Boats can be placed either horizontally or veritically

Boats can't be placed over each other

4. Turns

Players take turns "shooting" at their opponent's squares, trying to guess the position of enemy boats.

Each shot is indicated by selecting a square on the opponent's board.

The player who will "shoot" for the first time (first turn) will be selected at random.

5. Hits

If a player hits a square containing part of an enemy boat, a "hit" is scored.

If the shot does not hit any boat, a "miss" is marked

6. Sinking

When all of a boat's squares have been hit, the boat is considered "sunk".

The opponent will be informed which boat has been sunk.

7. Win Condition

The first player to sink the entire enemy fleet wins the game.

8. Turn Duration

Each turn will last no more than one minute (60 seconds). This is to ensure a good pacing of the game.

If a player does not make his move within the time limit, it is considered a lost turn.

Power-Ups

1. Sonar

Available as long as the submarine has not been sunk.

It will reveal a random tile of an enemy ship.

Cost: 5 points per use.

2. Attack Airplanes

Available as long as the carrier has not been sunk.

Shoots up to 5 random missiles at the enemy board.

Cost: 10 points per use.

3. Marine Mine

Players can plant a mine on their own board.

If the enemy attacks a mine, they receive an additional "hit" in a random adjacent square.

Cost: 5 points per use.

4. Defensive Shield

Protects a 3x3 region during 3 turns.

Attacks on the protected area are nullified and the enemy recieves a message saying their attacks were blocked.

Cost: 15 points.

Only one use per game.

5. Cruiser Missile

An attack directed at a 3x3 square area.

Cost: 15 points.

Recharge time: 5 turns.

6. Quick Fix

Restores up to 2 squares of a damaged (not sunken) boat.

Cost: 10 points.

Only one use per boat.

7. EMP Attack

Enemies won't be able to use power-ups for 3 turns.

Cost: 25 points.

Recharge time: 10 turns.

Extra Players

1. Game Format

Each player will have their own board and fleet of boats.

Players will take turns sequentially. The order of turns (who shoots first and who continues one after the other) will be chosen randomly before the game starts.

2. Turns

Turns will be managed in a circular order: player 1, player 2, player 3, player 4, and so on.

Each player shoots at a singular square of one the opponents' boards during their turn. This means that, in one turn, a player can only attack a single opponent.

A player cannot shoot at their own board.

3. Hits and Sinks

The rules for hits and sinks remain the same.

The game will inform all players when a boat has been sunk and who it belonged to.

4. Victory

The game continues until only one player remains with unsunk boats.

The last player to have boats on their board is the winner.

Tournament

1. Registration and Participation

Players must register to participate in the tournament.

Each tournament has a limit of 16 participants.

2. Tournament Format

Single elimination: winners advance to the next round.

Each match follows the basic rules mentioned beforehand, including the use of the previously mentioned power-ups.

3. Leaderboard

The leaderboard will automatically update with the results of each match.

It will show the progress of each player in the tournament.

4. Rewards and Recognition

Winners will receive virtual medals.

Champions will be highlighted in a special section of the leaderboard.
